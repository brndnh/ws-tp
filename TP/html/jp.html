days 1-14: japan

transit...

throughout most of my life beyond elementary school, i have been using vancouver's transit system, translink to get around the city whenever I wanted to get to places with my friends.

it was fascinating to me how I could just get across most of the city without having to drive a car. my friends in california and texas all drive everywhere to get anything done in comparison.

having been familiarized with how train stations and busses work back at home, I was overwhelmingly surprised by how efficient and just massive the metro systems were in Japan and to an extent, South Korea's subways.

it all moves like clockwork; they connect all over the gigantic city, and people just flow in and out of train stations that are way bigger than the ones here in vancouver.

aquariums

having grown up in a place where there was only one aquarium with really expensive tickets, the thought of viewing marine life just because never really crossed my mind.

this all changed when i was planning out my next day on the whim reading my travel booklet, and it suggested going to a local aquarium in tokyo. it was on this day that i acquired my profound interest towards sea mammals.
